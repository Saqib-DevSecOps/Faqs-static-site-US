1. What is Consolidated Billing? [AWS: Management: AWS Organizations, Billing]
    1. A billing option that enables customers to consolidate payment for multiple Amazon Web Services (AWS) accounts
    2. Consolidated billing is a feature leveraged by the AWS Organizations service to consolidate billing and payment for multiple AWS accounts.
    3. Every organization in AWS Organizations has a management (or payer) account that pays the charges of all the member accounts.
    4. The benefits of Consolidated Billing include:
      1. One bill – The management account is responsible for paying the charges of all the member accounts. The member accounts can see their cost and usage, however it is for informational purposes only.
      2. Easy tracking – The management account can use consolidated billing to view aggregated usage costs across multiple member accounts in the organization.
      3. Combined usage – The usage across all accounts in the organization, can be combined to share the volume pricing discounts, Reserved Instance discounts, and Savings Plans. This can result in a lower charge for your project, department, or company than with individual standalone accounts.
      4. No extra fee – Consolidated billing is offered at no additional cost.
    5. Note: "SCPs are not available in an organization in which only consolidated billing is enabled. For SCPs to be available, you must enable all features in the organization."
