1. What is the hierarchy in AWS Organizations: Ex: Org > Root > Management Account > Member Accounts > Org Unit. [AWS: Management: AWS Organizations, Hierarchy]
    1. The basic structure or hierarchy of AWS Organizations includes the following components, listed in a top down order:
      1. Organization - This is the central entity that consolidates the accounts, policies and other resources. An organization has one management account along with zero or more member accounts.
      2. Administrative Root - The administrative root is the top-most container in your organization’s hierarchy. It is the starting point for organizing the AWS accounts. It is a container for accounts, policies and other resources. You can create Organizational Units (OUs), and nested OUs under the administrative root to logically group your accounts and a hierarchy that best matches your business needs.Â  You can apply a service control policy (SCP) at the administrative root.
      3. Organizational unit (OU) - A container (or sub-container) for accounts within a root. You can create an OU hierarchy by having nested OUs. Policies attached at any point in the organization flow down and affect all OUs, nested OUs and member ccounts.
      4. Management Account - This is a standard AWS Root Account used to create the Organization service. It has responsibilities, such as owning all resources and acting as the payer account, responsible for paying all charges accrued by the member accounts.
      5. Member Account - This is a standard AWS Root Account, invited (or created) into the Organization by the management account. These are the accounts owned by the various teams and business units with the company.
      1. Service control policies (SCPs) are one of the policies that be applied within the Organization. They are similar to AWS Identity and Access Management (IAM) permission policies and use almost the same syntax.
        However, an SCP never grants permissions. SCPs are JSON policies that specify the maximum permissions that users and roles can exercise within the Organization.
        They limits what member accounts can do.
