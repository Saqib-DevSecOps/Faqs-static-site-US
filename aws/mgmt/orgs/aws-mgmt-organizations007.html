1. What is an AWS Organizations Service Control Policy (SCP)? [AWS: Management: AWS Organizations, Policy]
    1. SCPs define the maximum available permissions for IAM entities in  member accounts
      1. It defines the "outer boundaries" of what can be done in an account
    2. SCPs attach at multiple places in an AWS Organization: root, Organization Unital (OU), and individual member accounts
      1. Effects of SCPs apply at the level it's applied and down, e.g. at the root and down to any OUs and member accounts.
      2. When you apply a policy to the root, it applies to every OU and account in the organization.
      3. When you attach a policy to an OU, it applies only to that OU and all its accounts
      4. When you attach a policy to an account, it affects only that account
    3. Note: SCPs do not grant permissions
    4. "You can configure the service control policies (SCPs) in your organization to work as either of the following:
      1. A deny list – actions are allowed by default, and you specify what services and actions are prohibited. AWS Organizations attaches an AWS managed SCP named FullAWSAccess to every root and OU when it's created. This policy allows all services and actions. This is the default configuration of SCPs in AWS Organizations. "With a deny list strategy a default SCP allows all services and deny lists must be implemented for any specific services that must be restricted."
      2. An allow list – actions are prohibited by default, and you explicitly specify what services and actions are allowed. An allow list strategy has you remove the default AWS managed policy (FullAWSAccess) and replace it with an SCP that explicitly permits only those services and actions you want to allow, otherwise all actions for all services are implicitly denied.
    5. Note: "SCPs are not available in an organization in which only consolidated billing is enabled. For SCPs to be available, you must enable all features in the organization."
