1. I've come across this term undifferentiated heavy liftinga lot. What does it mean?
    2. [AWS: General: Information, Services]
    1. It points to things companies would do well to hand-off to someone else to perform because, while necessary/important:
      1. it is not core to the company's business
      2. it does not differentiate the business
      3. it might represent a low/negative ROI
    2. In 2006, Jeff Bezos, Amazon CEO at the time, called it the "the undifferentiated yet all-important heavy lifting needed to create and operate a web application."
    3. Depending on the AWS service being used by the customer, undifferentiated heavy-lifting activities. that customer can hand-off to AWS, might include one or more of the following:
      1. Negotiating contracts and licensing, hosting servers
      2. Installing and/or patching applications and/or operating systems
      3. Configuring the operating environments such as networking and firewalls, users and groups
      4. Creating scalable, reliable, secure, highly-available, and cost-effective architectures
      5. Coordinating the multiple teams needed to deploy and maintain the environment
      6. Etc.
    4. Instead of focusing on business infrastructure, companies and customers can focus on their business by leaving the undifferentiated heavy-lifting to AWS
    5. For example, customers no longer have to deal with the undifferentiated heavy-lifting of managing file servers and storage, as "Amazon FSx automates administration tasks such as hardware provisioning, software configuration, patching, and backups."
    6. Avoiding "undifferentiated heavy-lifting" allows the customer to spend less time supporting the IT infrastructure, and more time supporting the business.
