1. What is a hyperscaler in Cloud Computing? [AWS: General: Information]
    1. The name Hyperscale implies extreme growth
    2. "In computing, hyperscale is the ability of an architecture to scale appropriately as increased demand is added to the system." (Wikipedia)
    3. Hyperscale operators, or Hyperscalers are companies who offer IT, social media and other hosted services, on a broad scale. These include cloud service providers such as Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform, Alibaba Cloud, IBM Cloud, Facebook, Apple, Salesforce, etc.
    4. As described by IBM, "Hyperscaler: Provides computing architecture to appropriately scale as customers increase system demand. Hyperscaling typically involves seamlessly provisioning and adding compute, memory, networking, and storage resources to a given node or set of nodes that comprise a larger computing, distributed computing, or grid computing environment. Examples of hyperscalers are Amazon AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google GCP, Alibaba AliCloud, IBM, and Oracle."
    5. In 2017, Synergy Research Group said it identified 24 companies who met its definition of a hyperscaler.
    6. In 2021, the top three hyperscalers by market share are: Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Google Compute Platform.
