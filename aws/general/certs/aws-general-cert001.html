1. Any advice about taking the online proctored AWS Certification exam? [AWS: General: Certifications]
    1. Use your personal computer, not a work computer
    2. Computer with a webcam
    3. Be prepared to show a 360o view of the space you are in
    4. Your testing space should be private and unadorned; with no items that potentially could give you an unfair advantage, e.g. writings on the wall
    5. An online proctored exam must be completed in a continuous session as breaks will not be allowed
    6. Use the restroom ahead of time
    7. Login in 30 minutes advance
    8. Your test can be cancelled by the proctor if the conditions are not met, e.g. if anyone enters the room while you are taking the exam!
    9. With PSI, candidates must be able to communicate with the proctor in English, with Pearson VUE, candidates can opt for a Japanese-speaking proctor instead
    10. You may not be permitted to read the questions out loud
    11. Local writing material (scratch paper) is not allowed
    12. "There is no difference in the exams themselves when delivered in an online proctored modality."
    13. Both testing providers Pearson VUE and PSI offer both the online proctored and the on-site options. However confirm that the modality you want is available in your area, in the timeframe you want.
    14. Go to the AWS Certification page, and Schedule an exam
    15. Here's a message from Pearson OnVUE on the online proctored exam:
      16. "If you are taking an online proctored exam in an available country, we strongly recommend using a personal computer (not issued from your employer) and a wired, ethernet connection. The two most frequent causes of technical issues are testing on (1) a work computer and (2) an unreliable internet connection. Security settings from work computers may restrict the software. Unreliable internet connections (wireless network, VPN, mobile hotspot) may lead to being disconnected."
    1. Some tips I've picked up about online proctored exam:
      1. Online is better from a convenience POV, however, make sure the area isclean
      2. Testing facility is better from a simplicity POV, just show up with ID, and take test
      3. Make sure you can be undisturbed for the duration of the exam
      4. No one is allowed to walk into the room while you are testing - most of the time, they will end your test
      5. Test the software well in advance of the exam
      6. You'll download an app. Go to their page and test it out in advance
      7. No extra monitors and peripherals allowed
      8. Mac or PC, either is fine. It's mainly that you need to be able to show them the area is REALLY clean
      9. No speaking, swearing, or muttering acceptable, even if you are alone
      10. you can't even move your lips
      11. If you are someone who needs to mouth the words / read aloud to yourself, go into a testing center.
      12. Have your phone handy, however make sure its visible to the proctor. You may be asked to keep it out of easy reach
      13. It has facial recognition and will go red silhouette if you move out of frame
      14. Have your laptop/computer connected to a power supply
      15. This is a lot. at a facility just be prepared and show up and pass. Super easy in and out
