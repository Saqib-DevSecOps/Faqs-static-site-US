1. What's the difference between: simple scaling, step scaling and target tracking scaling? [AWS, Compute, Auto Scaling]
    1. Simple, Step, and Target Tracking scaling are examples of a Dynamic Scaling policy.

    1. "A dynamic scaling policy instructs Amazon EC2 Auto Scaling to track a specific CloudWatch metric, and it defines what action to take when the associated CloudWatch alarm is in ALARM."
    2. "Target tracking scaling—Increase or decrease the current capacity of the EC2 Auto Scaling group based on a target value for a specific metric.
      1. "With target tracking scaling policies, you select a scaling metric and set a target value.

        1. Amazon EC2 Auto Scaling creates and manages the CloudWatch alarms that trigger the scaling policy and calculates the scaling adjustment based on the metric and the target value. The scaling policy adds or removes capacity as required to keep the metric at, or close to, the specified target value."
        2. Target tracking works similar to a thermostat, automatically adjusting desired capacity to keep utilization at a constant level based on the metric and target value that you configure.
          1. For example, you can configure target tracking to keep the average CPU utilization for your fleet of web servers at 50 percent.
          2. Amazon EC2 Auto Scaling then launches or terminates EC2 instances as required to keep the aggregated CPU utilization across all servers at 50 percent
    1. "Step scaling policies and simple scaling policies are two of the dynamic scaling options available for you to use.
      1. Both Step scaling and Simple scaling —Increase or decrease the current capacity of the group. Step scaling does this based on a set of scaling adjustments, known as step adjustments, that vary based on the size of the alarm breach. Simple scaling does this based on a single scaling adjustment.
      2. Both require you to specify the high and low thresholds for the alarms in Amazon CloudWatch, and then define two scaling policies, one for scaling out (adding Instances) and the other for scaling in (removing Instances).
      3. Step scaling can make bigger or smaller size adjustments based on the metric value, while simple scaling always makes the same size adjustment. "
      4. "The main issue with simple scaling is that after a scaling activity is started, the policy must wait for the scaling activity or health check replacement to complete and the cooldown period to end before responding to additional alarms."
    2. "Both require you to  alarms. Both require you to define whether to add or remove instances, and how many, or set the group to an exact size."
    3. "If you are scaling based on a utilization metric that increases or decreases proportionally to the number of instances in an Auto Scaling group, we recommend that you use target tracking scaling policies. Otherwise, we recommend that you use step scaling policies."
