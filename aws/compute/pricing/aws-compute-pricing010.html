1. Can you keep track of your Reserved Instance usage? [AWS: Compute: Pricing]
    1. Yes
    2. AWS Budgets ca be used to track and take action on your AWS cost and usage. One of the things that AWS Budgets can monitor is the aggregate utilization and coverage metrics for your Reserved Instances (RIs) or Savings Plans.
    3. In the AWS Billing Console, you can use Budgets to track the utilization or coverage associated with your reservations and receive alerts when your percentage drops below a threshold you define. You can create the following RI-related budgets:
      1. RI utilization budgets
        1. Define a utilization threshold and receive alerts when your RI usage falls below that threshold.
        2. Setting a utilization target lets you see if your reservations (Reserved Instances) are unused or underutilized.
      2. RI coverage budgets
        1. Define a coverage threshold and receive alerts when the number of your instance hours that are covered by RIs fall below that threshold.
        2. Setting a coverage target lets you see how much of your instance usage is covered by reservations
    4. Reservation alerts are supported for:
      1. Amazon EC2
      2. Amazon RDS
      3. Amazon Redshift
      4. Amazon ElastiCache
      5. Amazon Elasticsearch reservations
