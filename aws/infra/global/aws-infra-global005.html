1. Does Netflix use Amazon CloudFront for its Content Delivery Network application? [AWS: Infrastructure: CloudFront, Edge Locations]
    1. According to Netflix they began their cloud migration in August 2008.
    2. Netflix chose Amazon Web Services as their cloud provider for AWS' scale and broad set of services and features.
    3. By 2016, Netflix had migrated all streaming services fully to the cloud, shutting down their last remaining related data center.
    4. In a 2016 blog post, Netflix touted its Netflix Open Connect service, its globally distributed CDN. They use it to deliver 100% of their video traffic.
    5. "Essentially everything before you hit play happens in AWS, including all of the logic of the application interface, the content discovery and selection experience, recommendation algorithms, transcoding, " online storage, recommendation engine, video transcoding, databases, analytics, etc.
    6. However Netflix operates its own system of partner ISPs (Internet Service Providers), networks, technical expertise and appliances called Netflix Open Connect to create a CDN service for the actual content streaming.
    7. A user on Quora.com put it very nicely, "Netflix uses AWS for their control plane. Anything that doesn’t involve serving video is handled in AWS. All video is streamed over Netflix’s purpose built Open Connect CDN (Content Delivery Network). That’s their data plane."
